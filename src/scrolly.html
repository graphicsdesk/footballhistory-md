Columbia wins their first Ivy League title with head coach Aldo Donelli. They go 6-1 in the League with their only loss coming to Princeton.



With a loss against Bucknell, the Lions begin their 44-game losing streak.

The Lawrence A. Wien Stadium, along with the Baker Field (now Kraft Field), opened during the losing streak and didn’t see a win until 1988.

A 16-13 win against Princeton marked the end of a 44 game losing streak – the NCAA record at the time.

Under head coach Ray Tellier the Lions went 8-2 overall with a 5-2 in conference record. The Lions finished second in the League behind Dartmouth.

In a 22-6 loss to Brown, the Light Blue ended the season on a loss. This continued for three years until the 2015 season and a win against Wagner University.



An Ivy League coaching legend from Penn, Al Bagnoli was announced as the new head coach of Columbia football. Bagnoli arrived to replace Pete Mangurian who left after three seasons with the team.

The 2017 season was the first for the Lions in 21 years. They ended with a record of 8-2 overall with five total Ivy wins.

Al Bagnoli’s sudden retirement allowed interim coach Mark Fabish to be announced in August prior to the start of the season. Despite his previous time alongside Bagnoli, Fabish only managed a 3-6 record with only one win in the Ivy League coming against Cornell.

In December of the same year, Union college head coach Jon Poppe was announced to replace Fabish. Poppe previously worked at Columbia from 2015-2017, and had also served time at Harvard.

Poppe’s 5-2 conference record was enough to clinch the Ivy League title for the second time in Columbia football history. This title comes after a 63 year span.
